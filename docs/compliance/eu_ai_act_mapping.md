# EU AI Act Compliance Mapping (SDK Capability Matrix)

> **Scope note:** This matrix maps currently implemented Proof Layer SDK capabilities to selected EU AI Act control areas. It is a technical feature map, not legal advice or a declaration of conformity.

## Control-to-Feature Matrix

| Control area | Implemented mechanism in SDK/runtime | Evidence produced by SDK | Known gaps requiring organizational process outside SDK |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Article 12 — logging / record-keeping** | Runtime capture of typed events (`CaptureEvent` / `EvidenceBundle`), deterministic canonicalization + hashing, Merkle root signing, optional RFC 3161 timestamping, optional transparency anchoring, optional vault retention/audit/export workflows. | Signed `bundle.pkg` (or redacted disclosure package), hash commitments for prompt/response and governance records, signature + verification artifacts, optional timestamp receipts and transparency receipts, vault audit-trail records where vault is used. | Log completeness policy (what must always be captured), legal retention schedules, jurisdiction-specific records management, access governance/SoD, operational monitoring of logger health, and regulator-facing record governance must be defined and enforced by the deploying organization. |
| **Article 14 — human oversight hooks** | First-class `human_oversight` and `policy_decision` evidence items; SDK helpers in Rust/TS/Python for capturing reviewer actions, overrides, anomaly/stop triggers, and rationale commitments; pack/profile readiness checks include oversight-related families. | Signed oversight records with reviewer/action metadata and commitment fields, linked policy-decision records, and readiness/completeness outputs showing presence/coverage for oversight evidence families. | The SDK does not provide staffing models, escalation chains, real-time intervention staffing, training/certification, or accountable role assignment. Organizations must implement operating procedures, RACI, and responder availability to make oversight effective in practice. |
| **Articles 9–10 — risk management + data governance touchpoints** | Structured `risk_assessment` and `data_governance` evidence item support; advisory completeness evaluation profiles (including governance-oriented profiles) to check minimally populated evidence families; support for attaching governance artefacts and commitments in sealed bundles. | Signed risk/data-governance entries (risk IDs, severity/status, mitigation summaries, dataset-governance metadata, etc.), immutable artefact commitments, and completeness evaluation reports indicating pass/fail for selected profile checks. | The SDK does not perform risk acceptance decisions, dataset qualification, representativeness validation, or bias remediation by itself. Organizations must run risk management lifecycle processes, data quality controls, and approvals (including human review and sign-off). |
| **Article 11 + Annex IV — technical documentation support** | Explicit evidence types for `technical_doc`, `instructions_for_use`, `qms_record`, `standards_alignment`, `post_market_monitoring`, plus Annex-IV-oriented readiness checks and disclosure/export pack support; deterministic packaging for third-party/offline verification. | Signed technical documentation commitments, machine-readable bundle manifests, pack-scoped completeness/readiness output, and disclosure packages suitable for sharing subsets with auditors/regulators. | Annex IV narrative quality, legal interpretation, and final technical file assembly remain organizational tasks. Teams must maintain document authoring governance, change control, and accountable approval workflows; SDK output supports evidence integrity but does not author the complete regulatory dossier automatically. |
| **Incident reconstruction + reporting readiness** | Incident-response/post-market evidence types (`incident_report`, `corrective_action`, `authority_notification`, `authority_submission`, `reporting_deadline`, `regulator_correspondence`), plus profile-based completeness checks and vault export/disclosure options. Cryptographic sealing supports post-hoc reconstruction integrity. | Time-ordered, signed incident evidence records with commitments to reports/messages, status/deadline tracking fields, and completeness outputs for incident-response or post-market monitoring profiles. | Breach/incident triage SOPs, legal notification determinations, regulator communication ownership, and mandatory reporting SLA enforcement must be run operationally by the organization. The SDK provides evidence plumbing, not automated legal adjudication or guaranteed deadline compliance. |

## Practical interpretation

- The SDK is strongest at **integrity, traceability, and portable evidence packaging**.
- It provides structured hooks for governance workflows, but **organizational controls** (policy, staffing, legal review, accountability, and operations) are required to close compliance obligations end-to-end.
- Readiness/completeness profiles are **advisory guardrails** for evidence presence/shape; they are not themselves legal conformity determinations.
